Policies

Premiere Program

Performers that give a performance of any of my unpremiered works may receive reimbursement for their music costs, and may be eligible for additional compensation depending on performance quality. Potential compensation and reimbursement are contingent on the creation of a publicly available recording of the newly premiered work. The recording must be of a sufficiently high quality, both from the performers and from the recording technology. Generally, more demanding works will also demand a higher quality of playing and recording.

Please keep in mind that many of my unpremiered works will need to be revised before they get sent. This means that a performer requesting to premiere a piece may have to wait several weeks or months before receiving their music, depending on my personal schedule and length of work. If you wish to premiere a work, do not wait. Please fill out the form at the end of this page. Only works marked as "Unpremiered" in the database above are eligible.

This policy is subject to modification over time.

Scoring Adjustments

In the case of large ensemble works, such as ones for string orchestra or full symphony orchestra, scoring modifications, reductions, and expansions are available. If you wish to request a scoring modification, please use my contact form on the homepage and explain what modifications you would like. If accepted, you will receive a quote for the cost of the modifications.

Certain modifications, such as changing the pitch of trumpet and clarinet parts for the entire score or parts of the score, are available free of charge. More complicated modifications, such as removing or adding instruments, may incur additional charges. Requests may be denied.

Photocopying

The act of photocopying my music or distributing photocopies of my music is prohibited, unless done to "fill out" a section of players. For example, it is permitted to purchase one copy of a Violin I, Violin II, Viola, Cello, or Double Bass part and photocopy for the rest of the section.
